Why I Love Working on Cars

Growing up, working on cars was never something I imagined I would enjoy or even like. In fact, I always disliked the idea of getting dirty and made an effort to stay as clean as possible while completing tasks. However, that all changed during my senior year of high school when I bought my first car. It was something I had worked incredibly hard for in the years leading up to it, saving every bit of extra money I could earn. Like many people experience when buying their first car, it felt almost like falling in love—seeing all your hard-earned money turn into something tangible and personal.

For me, this sparked a desire to make my car look and perform better than it did when I first got it, at least in my eyes. Fortunately, most of my friends were also in the process of getting their first cars, and some had already gotten theirs. This shared experience not only strengthened our friendships but also gave us a common goal: to modify our cars to reflect our personalities and preferences. Along the way, we learned so much—things we never knew before—about cars, mechanics, and even problem-solving. What started as a simple purchase turned into a passion and a journey of growth, both individually and as a group.
